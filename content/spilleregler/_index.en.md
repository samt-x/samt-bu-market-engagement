To ensure the open market dialogue is useful for everyone and legally sound, the following rules apply.

## How to contribute

You can contribute in several ways:

- **Suggest a change** – click "Suggest change" at the top of a page to edit content directly. Your change becomes a proposal that we review and comment on openly.
- **Suggest a new chapter** – the same mechanism applies for adding a new page.
- **Leave a comment** – smaller submissions can be added via the comment function at the top of the page.

You may contribute in Norwegian or English.

## Rights to submissions

By contributing here, you accept that your submission is publicly available and may be used by the contracting authority in drafting requirements specifications, tender documents, and further work, without any claim to compensation or specific consent beyond this.

Do not include trade secrets or confidential information here. This is an open arena. If you have something that must be kept confidential, contact us directly and we will arrange a separate process.

## Equal treatment

All submissions are visible to everyone. We (the contracting authority) consolidate submissions and create a comprehensive requirements specification based on the breadth of contributions – not any single supplier's draft. Contributing here confers no advantage in a subsequent competition.

## Phases

A market dialogue here will typically proceed through these phases:

1. **Opening** – problem statement and invitation are published
2. **Input period** – the market contributes submissions, edits, and proposals
3. **Summary** – we consolidate what has been received and what it means for further work
4. **Closing** – the dialogue is formally concluded; further process follows ordinary procurement channels

Each specific dialogue will have clearly stated status and deadlines.

## Conflicts of interest

Advisers and consultants who contribute here and who later wish to participate in a competition based on the dialogue should be aware of conflict-of-interest considerations. Contributing to an open market dialogue does not disqualify from later participation, but may in some cases trigger mitigating measures. Contact us if you are uncertain.
