## What is market dialogue?

Market dialogue is communication between a contracting authority and the market *before* a procurement is announced. The aim is not to negotiate a specific contract, but to understand what is technically and commercially feasible, to clarify needs, and to let the market help shape the problem statement.

For complex and innovative procurements, market dialogue is not only permitted, but strongly recommended – and in practice almost essential.

## Why open and written?

Traditional market dialogue often takes place through meetings, RFIs (Requests for Information), or bilateral conversations. This works, but has some weaknesses:

- Information is distributed unevenly – some suppliers learn more than others
- It is difficult to document that the equal treatment principle has been upheld
- Submissions get lost in email threads and remain inaccessible to others
- Small actors with good ideas are excluded because they are not on the distribution list

By conducting dialogue through open, version-controlled documents, we address several of these problems simultaneously. Everyone sees the same thing, everything is traceable, and the barrier to contribute is low.

## Legal grounding

Market dialogue is anchored in Norwegian procurement regulations and is particularly recommended for innovative procurements and research and development. The fundamental principles of equal treatment, transparency, and verifiability (Norwegian Public Procurement Act § 4) are not merely observed in this approach – they are built into the method.

## Relationship to specific procurements

An open market dialogue here does not replace the procurement process itself. When we move from dialogue to competition, this is done in the usual way – typically through announcement on Doffin. The dialogue is closed prior to announcement, and further communication takes place through the formal channels.

We may conduct multiple parallel or sequential dialogues over time, linked to different phases or sub-areas of SAMT-BU.
